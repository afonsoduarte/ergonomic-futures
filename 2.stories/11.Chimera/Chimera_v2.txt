What makes a human distinctively so?

The ingredient is so protected—the trade secret so valuable—that Francis Fukuyama can, at most, call it "Factor X."  In his 2002 book, _Our Posthuman Future_, the political scientist reverse-engineers the human, seeking essence by elimination.  Neither sentience nor reason, moral choice nor language, consciousness nor emotionality can account for our humanity.  But combine them just so, and we attain our ineffable factor.
